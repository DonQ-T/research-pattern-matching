\begin{frame}{The 4-Timepoint Problem}
\small
Each gene's trajectory has 3 transitions ($t_0 \to t_3$, $t_3 \to t_6$, $t_6 \to t_9$). Collapse each to its sign $\Rightarrow$ only $2^3 = 8$ possible ``shape classes.'' All 6 normalized-first methods also erase magnitude, so they're sorting $\sim\!17{,}856$ genes into 8 buckets on shape alone. Non-trivial fraction of genes share the constraint's sign class \textit{by accident}.
\normalsize
\begin{center}
\includegraphics[width=0.98\textwidth]{plots/sign_pattern_distribution.png}
\end{center}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Appendix --- Why Bother?}
\footnotesize
Only \textbf{Pearson} and \textbf{Cosine} are scale-invariant by definition. DTW, Fr\'echet, MSE, and sMAPE are genuine distance metrics --- they only \textit{behaved} as shape-based in the April 9 pipeline because every gene was normalized to $(0,1]$ before they saw it.\\[0.5em]
This appendix recomputes those four methods on \textbf{raw LT data} (no normalization) to see what their natural picks look like. Raw MAE is included for reference.
\normalsize
\vspace{1em}
\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lcccc}
\toprule
\textbf{Cluster} & \textbf{DTW} & \textbf{Fr\'echet} & \textbf{MSE} & \textbf{sMAPE} \\
\midrule
clusterOne   & 17/20 &  9/20 & 14/20 & 17/20 \\
clusterTwo   & 20/20 & 17/20 & 18/20 &  6/20 \\
clusterThree & 20/20 & 13/20 & 16/20 & 17/20 \\
clusterFour  &  7/20 &  4/20 & 17/20 &  0/20 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}\\[0.4em]
\footnotesize Top-20 overlap with raw MAE (both computed on unnormalized LT data).
\end{center}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Appendix --- Takeaway}
\begin{itemize}\itemsep8pt
  \item \textbf{DTW and MSE on raw $\approx$ raw MAE.} On 4 timepoints, their extra machinery (temporal warping, squared penalty) doesn't distinguish them meaningfully from pointwise MAE.
  \item \textbf{Fr\'echet is noisier.} Max-deviation focus punishes one bad timepoint, so it picks different outliers.
  \item \textbf{sMAPE stands apart.} Percentage-style denominator breaks down when pattern values are near zero --- erratic across clusters.
  \item \textbf{Practical point:} if magnitude-awareness is what matters, \textbf{raw MAE} is the cleanest and most interpretable form of this family.
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}
